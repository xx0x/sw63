# Languages

People have been saying the time in different ways across different languages and countries. Today, many people simply read time in a digital form, like "seventeen twenty-five" or "six forty", mostly because digital clocks are everywhere on our phones, computers, and other screens.

Older time expressions (most significantly in Central Europe languages) were shaped much more by analog clocks and by the way their hands move around the dial. The main differences are which hour the quarter, half, and three-quarter landmarks belong to, and how the sentence is structured around them.

The examples below use 17:25 and 9:17 to show how the same moment is interpreted by each language family. Some of the explanations are a bit of a stretch, but the whole thing is meant as a small experiment. The PM marker, if used, is always last.

Feel free to suggest additional languages and improvements.

## 1. Czech, Slovak, Ukrainian, some east Germany regions

**17:25**  
*before - 5 - minutes - half of - 6 - hours - PM*

**9:17**  
*quarter of - 10 - hours - and - 2 - minutes*

Hour shifts: first quarter = +1 (next hour), half = +1 (next hour), third quarter = +1 (next hour)  
Example: Czech "za pět minut půl šesté"  
Literal meaning: "in five minutes half of six"

## 2. English

**17:25**  
*5 - minutes - before - half past - 5 hours - PM*

**9:17**  
*quarter past - 9 - hours - and - 2 - minutes*

Hour shifts: first quarter = +0 (current hour), half = +0 (current hour), third quarter = +1 (next hour)  
Example: "five minutes before half past five"

## 3. German, Dutch

**17:25**  
*5 - minutes - before - half of - 6 - hours - PM*

**9:17**  
*quarter past - 9 - hours - and - 2 - minutes*

Hour shift: first quarter = +0 (current hour), half = +1 (next hour), third quarter = +1 (next hour)  
Examples: German "fünf vor halb sechs", Dutch "vijf voor half zes"  
Literal meaning: "five before half six"

## 4. Polish

**17:25**  
*before - 5 - minutes - half of - 6 - hours - PM*

**9:17**  
*2 - minutes - after - quarter past - 9 - hours*

Hour shift: first quarter = +0 (current hour), half = +1 (next hour), third quarter = +1 (next hour)  
Example: "za pięć wpół do szóstej"  
Literal meaning: "in five, half to six"

## 5. Hungarian

**17:25**  
*5 - minutes - before - half of - 6 - hours - PM*

**9:17**  
*and - 2 - minutes - quarter of - 10 - hours*

Hour shift: first quarter = +1 (next hour), half = +1 (next hour), third quarter = +1 (next hour)  
Example: "öt perc múlva fél hat"  
Literal meaning: "in five minutes, half six"

## 6. Danish, Norwegian, Swedish, Finnish

**17:25**  
*5 - minutes - before - half of - 6 - hours - PM*

**9:17**  
*2 - minutes - after - quarter past - 9 - hours*

Hour shift: first quarter = +0 (current hour), half = +1 (next hour), third quarter = +1 (next hour)  
Example: Danish "fem i halv seks" / "fem minutter i halv seks"  
Literal meaning: approximately "five into half six"
